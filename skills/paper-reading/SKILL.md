# Paper Reading Skill

Use this skill when the task involves reading an academic paper or turning a PDF/text paper into a structured research note.

## Workflow

1. Identify basic information: title, authors, venue/year when available, keywords, and paper source.
2. Extract the research background: what problem the paper studies and why it matters.
3. State the research gap: what prior work cannot handle, and what the paper claims as its contribution.
4. Summarize the method: core idea, model/system design, algorithmic flow, and important assumptions.
5. Summarize experiments: datasets, baselines, metrics, ablations, and main results.
6. Identify limitations: explicit limitations, weak assumptions, missing experiments, or external validity risks.
7. Generate research discussion questions that examine novelty, evidence, assumptions, and possible extensions.
8. End with a concise research summary grounded in the extracted evidence.

## Output Shape

Prefer Markdown notes with these sections:

- Basic Info
- Research Background
- Research Gap
- Method
- Experiments
- Results and Conclusion
- Limitations
- Research Discussion Questions
- Research Follow-up Ideas
- Concise Research Summary

## Reading Stance

Be useful but cautious. If a section is not clearly present in the extracted text, say so instead of inventing details. Separate direct paper evidence from your own inference.
